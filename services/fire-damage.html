Fire Damage Assessment

Evidence-based evaluation of how fire and elevated temperatures have affected your structure — establishing what remains safe, what needs repair, and what must be replaced.

Request an Assessment All Services
Introduction

Fire exposure can significantly alter the properties of construction materials, particularly concrete and steel. Even when visible damage appears minimal, the intense heat generated during a fire can weaken structural elements from within — reducing strength, changing material behaviour, and compromising the long-term performance of the structure. The critical question after any fire is rarely how a structure looks, but how it will behave under load in the years that follow.

CDE's fire damage assessments answer that question with evidence rather than assumption. Our engineers evaluate the impact of elevated temperatures on structural elements, quantify the extent of the damage sustained, and establish whether targeted repair, strengthening, or full replacement is the appropriate course of action. The result is a clear, defensible basis for restoring a structure safely and economically.

How Fire Affects a Structure

Concrete and steel respond to heat in ways that are not always apparent at surface level. Concrete may discolour, crack, and spall as moisture is driven off and the cement matrix breaks down, while its compressive strength can fall well before any obvious distress is visible. Steel reinforcement and structural sections lose strength and stiffness at elevated temperatures and may not fully recover on cooling. Because these effects vary with the intensity and duration of exposure, two elements that look identical can have very different residual capacity — which is precisely why a structured investigation is essential.

What a Fire Damage Assessment Covers

Each assessment is scoped to the structure and the severity of the fire, but a typical investigation follows a five-step process:

  • Visual inspection & damage mapping — systematic examination of discoloration, cracking, spalling, and deformation to record the pattern and extent of heat-related distress.
  • Material testing — sampling and laboratory analysis to measure changes in strength and material properties caused by the fire.
  • Heat exposure analysis — evaluation of the intensity and duration of fire exposure to identify the affected zones and the peak temperatures likely reached.
  • Structural evaluation — assessment of residual structural performance to determine whether elements remain safe and serviceable.
  • Repair strategy development — clear recommendations for the appropriate repair, strengthening, or replacement measures.

Why It Matters

Reoccupying or reinstating a fire-affected structure without a proper assessment carries real risk. Elements that appear sound may have lost significant capacity, while others showing surface distress may in fact remain structurally adequate. A rigorous evaluation distinguishes cosmetic damage from genuine loss of strength, preventing both unsafe reuse and the unnecessary cost of over-cautious demolition. It gives owners, insurers, and consultants a technical foundation for confident decisions on repair versus replacement, and a clear route back to full structural performance.

Our Approach

CDE works as an independent engineering consultancy, so our findings reflect the true condition of the structure rather than the interests of any repair contractor or product supplier. Every fire damage assessment is engineer-led from site to report, combining detailed inspection, targeted testing, and structural analysis into a single, actionable set of recommendations. Whether the damage affects a residential or commercial building, an industrial facility, or fire-affected infrastructure, our goal is the same: to restore structural integrity safely, and to do so with the minimum intervention the evidence allows.
