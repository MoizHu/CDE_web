Thermal Modeling

Predictive simulation of temperature distribution within concrete structures to anticipate thermal stresses, evaluate cracking risk, and optimise designs before construction begins.

Request an Assessment All Services
Introduction

Temperature variations within concrete structures can lead to thermal stresses, which may result in cracking and long-term durability issues. These effects are most pronounced in large structural elements and under extreme environmental conditions — precisely the circumstances that define much of the construction work carried out across the Middle East. Thermal modeling gives engineers a way to anticipate this behaviour rather than react to it once cracks appear.

CDE uses thermal modeling as a predictive tool during the design and construction phases. By simulating temperature distribution within a structure, we are able to understand its impact on structural behaviour and durability, and to recommend measures that reduce the risk of early-age and long-term cracking before a single cubic metre of concrete is placed.

What Thermal Modeling Involves

Thermal modeling is the process of simulating temperature distribution within a structure to understand its impact on structural behaviour and durability. It brings together material science, environmental data, and construction planning to reveal how heat develops, moves, and dissipates through a member over time — and where that movement is likely to generate damaging stress.

Our Methodology

Each study is tailored to the structure and its environment, but our approach follows four connected stages:

  • Data collection — gathering and analysing material properties, environmental conditions, and the planned construction sequence that together govern thermal behaviour.
  • Simulation — employing advanced modeling tools to simulate temperature variations over time throughout the structure.
  • Stress analysis — evaluating the resulting thermal stresses and the associated risk of cracking within each element.
  • Design optimisation — recommending design and construction modifications that minimise thermal effects and protect long-term durability.

Scope & Applications

Our services cover thermal analysis of structures, temperature-variation simulation, crack-risk evaluation, and design-improvement recommendations. This work is particularly valuable for mass concrete structures, foundations and slabs, infrastructure projects, and high-temperature environments, where the volume of concrete and the surrounding climate combine to make heat of hydration a genuine engineering concern.

Why It Matters

The benefit of thermal modeling is proactive control. By understanding thermal behaviour in advance, owners and designers gain a reduced risk of thermal cracking, improved structural durability, and designs that are optimised for the conditions they will actually face. Thermal modeling enables proactive design decisions, reducing the risk of early-age and long-term cracking — protecting both the integrity and the service life of the asset from the outset.
