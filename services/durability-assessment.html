Introduction

Durability plays a critical role in determining how long a structure can perform under the environmental and operational conditions it faces. In the Middle East, where high temperatures, aggressive chlorides, and intense exposure place exceptional demands on concrete, durability is not a detail to be assumed — it is a property that must be evaluated, quantified, and planned for from the outset.

CDE's durability assessment and service life prediction services give owners, developers, and consultants a clear picture of both the current condition of a structure and how it is likely to behave over time. By combining condition insight with forward-looking performance analysis, we help you make informed decisions early — before minor deterioration escalates into costly repair and shortened service life.

What a Durability Assessment Covers

A durability assessment examines the factors that govern how a structure will resist deterioration across its intended life. Rather than reacting to distress after it appears, the assessment builds an evidence base for long-term performance. A typical scope includes:

  • Exposure evaluation — characterising the environmental and operational conditions the structure will face, from marine and buried environments to aggressive industrial exposure.
  • Material & condition review — assessing concrete quality, cover, and the properties that determine resistance to deterioration mechanisms.
  • Deterioration analysis — identifying the mechanisms most likely to threaten the structure, such as reinforcement corrosion, chloride ingress, and carbonation.
  • Service life prediction — modelling future performance to estimate how long the structure can perform before intervention is required.
  • Findings & recommendations — a clear report translating the analysis into practical measures that protect durability and extend service life.

Service Life Prediction

Service life prediction looks beyond present condition to forecast future performance. By evaluating how a structure will respond to sustained environmental and operational stressors, we estimate the time before deterioration reaches a threshold that demands intervention. This forward view turns durability from an abstract expectation into a measurable, plannable outcome — allowing design choices, protective measures, and maintenance budgets to be set against a realistic performance horizon.

Why It Matters

Deterioration rarely announces itself. It often progresses gradually and invisibly, and without proper evaluation it can advance unnoticed until repairs become extensive and expensive. Assessing durability and predicting service life early prevents minor issues from escalating into major structural and maintenance problems, protecting both investment value and the safety of those who use the structure. For new structures in particular, it is the point at which durability can still be shaped rather than merely repaired.

Our Approach

CDE works as an independent engineering consultancy, free from commercial bias toward any particular product or repair method. Our durability assessments are engineer-led and grounded in the real exposure conditions of the region, so our conclusions reflect the structure's genuine needs. Every recommendation is framed around one objective: achieving the intended service life safely, durably, and cost-effectively.
