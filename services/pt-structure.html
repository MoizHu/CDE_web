PT Structure Assessment

Specialist evaluation of post-tensioned structures — assessing tendons, anchorages, and structural behaviour to uncover corrosion, loss of prestress, and hidden risk before it threatens safety.

Request an Assessment All Services
Introduction

Post-tensioned structures are widely used across the region for their structural efficiency and performance, allowing longer spans, slimmer sections, and more economical designs than conventional reinforced concrete. Yet the very features that make them so effective also make them uniquely demanding to evaluate. Their behaviour depends on high-stress tendons and anchorages that are largely concealed within the structure, and any deterioration within the post-tensioning system carries significant safety implications.

PT structure assessment is the specialist evaluation of the condition and performance of these post-tensioning systems — including the tendons, anchorages, and the overall structural behaviour they govern. Because the critical components are hidden and highly stressed, they require investigation techniques and engineering judgement well beyond those used for ordinary concrete. CDE brings that specialist capability, combining targeted inspection and testing with rigorous structural analysis to give owners a clear, evidence-based understanding of how their post-tensioned structures are performing.

Why Post-Tensioned Structures Demand Specialist Assessment

In a post-tensioned structure, load-carrying capacity relies on the integrity of tendons held under sustained high stress. Corrosion, water ingress at anchorages, grouting defects, or a loss of prestress can quietly compromise that capacity long before any outward sign appears. Because these mechanisms develop out of view, a conventional visual survey alone cannot confirm whether a PT system is sound. A structured, specialist assessment is the only reliable way to distinguish a healthy structure from one carrying concealed risk.

Our Assessment Approach

CDE follows a disciplined, engineer-led methodology that moves from observation through to a defensible remedial strategy. Each assessment is scoped to the structure and the concerns at hand, but a typical investigation progresses through five phases:

  • Visual inspection — systematic examination of accessible PT components to identify signs of distress, cracking, water ingress, or damage at anchorages and along tendon runs.
  • Testing & evaluation — specialist techniques applied to assess tendon condition, detect corrosion, and identify any loss of prestress within the system.
  • Structural analysis — evaluation of how the structure is actually behaving against its design intent to identify deficiencies and quantify their significance.
  • Risk assessment — identification and evaluation of the potential risks associated with the PT system, prioritised by their impact on safety and service life.
  • Repair strategy development — recommendation of appropriate, proportionate repair or strengthening solutions matched to the findings.

Scope of Service

Our PT assessments cover the full chain of components and behaviours that determine how a post-tensioned structure performs. This includes inspection of the PT system as a whole, evaluation of tendon condition, assessment of corrosion, structural analysis of observed behaviour, and clear, actionable repair recommendations. The scope is always tailored so that the investigation focuses effort where the risk is greatest.

The Value It Delivers

A well-executed PT assessment turns uncertainty into a basis for confident decisions. By identifying hidden risks early, verifying the integrity of concealed tendons and anchorages, and framing effective repair strategies, the assessment improves safety and extends the service life of the structure. For owners, consultants, and facility managers responsible for post-tensioned assets, it provides the technical assurance needed to plan maintenance, protect occupants, and safeguard long-term value.
