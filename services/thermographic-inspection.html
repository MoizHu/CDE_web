Thermographic Inspection

Infrared thermal imaging that reveals hidden moisture, insulation failures, and energy loss — exposing defects invisible to the naked eye without touching the structure.

Request an Assessment All Services
Introduction

Some of the most damaging defects in a building never appear on its surface. Moisture tracking behind a façade, insulation that has failed within a wall, or heat escaping through a compromised roof can all progress for years before any visible sign emerges. Thermographic inspection uses infrared imaging to detect the subtle temperature variations these problems create, giving engineers a way to see what conventional visual inspection cannot.

CDE deploys thermal imaging as a fast, non-destructive diagnostic tool across building envelopes, roofs, and mechanical and electrical systems. By translating invisible temperature differences into clear thermal maps, we locate hidden anomalies precisely — allowing owners to intervene early, before minor defects develop into costly failures.

How Thermographic Inspection Works

Thermography reads the heat signature of a structure. Because moisture, air gaps, and insulation loss all change how a material holds and releases heat, they register as distinct patterns on an infrared image. Our engineers survey the structure with calibrated thermal cameras, then interpret the results against the building's construction and environmental conditions to separate genuine defects from harmless thermal noise. Each survey follows a disciplined sequence:

  • Infrared survey — the structure is scanned with thermal imaging equipment to capture temperature differences across its surfaces.
  • Data interpretation — thermal images are analysed to identify anomalies and distinguish potential defects from normal variation.
  • Defect identification — areas affected by moisture intrusion, insulation failure, or energy loss are pinpointed and mapped.
  • Reporting — findings are documented with annotated images and clear technical explanations that support decision-making.

What It Detects

Thermographic inspection is especially effective at exposing conditions that leave no visible trace. It reliably identifies moisture and leakage intrusion, insulation defects and failures, patterns of energy loss, and hidden structural issues that would otherwise go unnoticed until they caused visible or costly damage.

Where We Apply It

Because the technique is entirely non-contact, it suits a wide range of assets and inspection goals. CDE applies thermographic inspection across building envelopes, roof systems, and electrical and mechanical installations, as well as in energy efficiency assessments where understanding heat flow is central to the investigation.

Why It Matters

The value of thermography lies in seeing problems early and without damage. Detecting hidden defects before they escalate allows timely, targeted intervention, improves energy efficiency, and reduces long-term maintenance costs. As a fully non-destructive method, it delivers this insight without cutting, coring, or otherwise disturbing the structure — making it an efficient first line of diagnosis for any building under investigation.

Our Approach

CDE works as an independent engineering consultancy, so our thermographic findings reflect the structure's real condition rather than any commercial agenda. Every survey is engineer-led from site to report, and thermal results are interpreted in the context of the wider assessment — combined, where useful, with other diagnostic testing to build a complete and defensible picture of the asset's health.
