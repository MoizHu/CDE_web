Building Condition Audit

A systematic inspection and risk-based evaluation of your building that reveals its true condition and gives you a clear, defensible basis for maintenance decisions.

Request an Audit All Services
Introduction

Buildings deteriorate gradually — through environmental exposure, daily use, and the simple passage of time. Left unchecked, minor issues quietly compound, until small defects have grown into major structural and maintenance problems that are far more disruptive and expensive to resolve. Regular evaluation is the only reliable way to stay ahead of that curve.

A building condition audit gives property owners a clear, evidence-based picture of their asset's current state. By systematically inspecting the structure and the components that affect its performance, the audit supports better decisions about where to direct maintenance investment and how to preserve the asset over the long term. CDE delivers these audits as an independent engineering exercise, focused entirely on the building's real needs.

What a Building Condition Audit Covers

A building condition audit is a systematic inspection and evaluation of a structure to identify defects, assess risks, and determine maintenance requirements. It looks beyond the primary structural frame to include the related components that affect a building's performance and safety. A typical audit includes:

  • Structural and element inspection — detailed examination of structural and related elements to locate cracks, spalling, leakage, and other signs of deterioration.
  • Defect identification & classification — cataloguing every observed defect and grouping it by type and severity.
  • Condition evaluation — assessment of each component's status, the extent of any damage, and the risks it presents.
  • Risk evaluation & prioritisation — ranking of defects according to their impact on safety, performance, and durability.
  • Maintenance recommendations — practical repair and maintenance guidance, delivered in a structured audit report.

Our Approach

CDE follows a disciplined, five-step methodology on every audit. We begin with a comprehensive inspection of the structural elements to locate defects such as cracking, spalling, leakage, and other signs of deterioration. Each finding then passes through a condition evaluation that establishes the component's status, the severity of any damage, and the risks involved.

From there we carry out a formal risk assessment, weighing each defect against its impact on safety, performance, and durability, before documenting the findings in a structured record with clear descriptions and supporting data. Finally, that documentation feeds directly into maintenance planning input — a set of recommendations designed to guide the owner's repair and maintenance strategy.

Why It Matters

Deterioration rarely announces itself until it is well advanced, and by then the cost of intervention has multiplied. A structured audit catches problems early, separates critical risks from cosmetic ones, and gives owners a clear understanding of their building's condition. The outcome is improved maintenance planning, better-directed spending, and an extended service life for the asset.

Where It Applies

Building condition audits suit a wide range of assets and programmes — commercial and residential buildings, industrial facilities, institutional buildings, and structured asset management portfolios. Whether you are preparing for a transaction, responding to visible distress, or setting a long-term maintenance budget, the audit provides the technical foundation for every decision that follows.
