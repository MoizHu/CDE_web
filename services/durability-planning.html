Durability Planning

Designing durability into new structures from the outset — through exposure assessment, material specification, cover detailing, and protection strategy — so your asset achieves its intended service life with minimal maintenance.

Discuss Your Project All Services
Introduction

Durability is a fundamental requirement for any structure expected to perform over its intended service life. Yet many of the problems that surface years after handover arise not from poor construction, but from a lack of proper durability considerations during the design stage. By the time deterioration appears, the opportunity to prevent it economically has usually passed — the choices that govern how long a structure lasts are made long before the first pour.

Durability planning is the process of integrating durability requirements into the design and construction stages of a project. It focuses on ensuring that a structure can achieve its intended service life with minimal maintenance, by anticipating the environment it will face and building the right resistance into the concrete, the reinforcement, and the detailing from day one. For new structures in the Middle East — where aggressive coastal, marine, and industrial exposures are the norm — this discipline is the single most cost-effective investment an owner can make in the long-term performance of an asset.

Designing Durability In

Where a condition assessment looks backward at how an existing structure has performed, durability planning looks forward. It translates the intended service life — often 50, 75, or 100 years — into concrete engineering decisions taken while the design is still on the drawing board. CDE works alongside owners, architects, and structural engineers to define those decisions early, so that durability is designed in rather than corrected later.

Our involvement typically begins during concept or detailed design, when there is still full freedom to shape the specification. We assess the service environment, select materials matched to it, and set out the detailing and quality-control criteria needed to deliver the design intent on site.

Exposure Assessment

Every durability strategy starts with an honest reading of the environment the structure will live in. CDE evaluates the environmental conditions acting on the asset — coastal and marine exposure, airborne and ground-borne chlorides, industrial pollution, humidity, and the severe temperature and wetting-drying cycles characteristic of the region. From this we assign appropriate exposure classes to each element, recognising that a splash-zone marine wall, a buried foundation, and a sheltered internal slab face very different risks and warrant very different provisions.

Key Elements of a Durability Plan

Each plan is tailored to the structure and its environment, but a comprehensive durability strategy generally addresses:

  • Exposure assessment — classifying each structural element against the coastal, marine, industrial, and climatic conditions it will face throughout its life.
  • Material selection — specifying concrete quality, cement type, supplementary cementitious materials, and reinforcement protection matched to the assessed exposure and required performance.
  • Cover and detailing — defining reinforcement cover, crack-width limits, joint arrangements, and drainage detailing that keep aggressive agents away from the steel.
  • Protection strategy — where warranted, layering additional defences such as coatings, membranes, corrosion inhibitors, or cathodic prevention to extend service life in the most demanding zones.
  • Performance criteria — setting clear, measurable acceptance criteria that guide construction quality control and verify the durability design has actually been delivered.

Why It Matters

A structure designed without durability in mind may satisfy every strength and stability check and still fall short of its intended life, demanding costly repairs and disruptive interventions far sooner than expected. Durability planning closes that gap. By investing modest engineering effort at the design stage, owners secure longevity, reduce whole-life maintenance costs, and avoid the far larger expense of remediating deterioration once it has taken hold — an outcome that is especially valuable for coastal and marine structures, critical infrastructure, and industrial facilities in high-exposure settings.

Our Approach

CDE works as an independent engineering consultancy, so the durability provisions we recommend reflect the structure's real needs rather than any product or supplier. Our specialists translate exposure into specification, specification into buildable detailing, and detailing into verifiable performance criteria — then remain available through construction to ensure the durability intent survives the realities of site. The result is a structure engineered, from its first line on the drawing board, to last.
