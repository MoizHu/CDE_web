Repair Supervision & QA/QC

Independent site supervision and quality assurance that make sure a well-engineered repair is actually built the way it was designed.

Request an Assessment All Services
Introduction

The effectiveness of any repair or rehabilitation project depends not only on its design, but on the quality of its execution. A sound specification counts for little if surface preparation is rushed, the wrong material is used, or curing is neglected. Poor workmanship and inadequate monitoring can quietly undermine even the most carefully engineered solution — and the resulting failures rarely appear until the repair is already in service.

CDE's repair supervision and QA/QC services close that gap between design and delivery. As an independent engineering consultancy, we oversee the works on site, verify that every material and process meets the specification, and document the entire operation so that owners and consultants can be confident the finished repair will perform as intended over its full design life.

What Our Supervision Covers

Our role on site is to protect the design intent at every stage of the works. A typical supervision and QA/QC scope includes:

  • Site supervision — continuous on-site monitoring to ensure the works are carried out in accordance with the approved design and method statements.
  • Material verification — checking that all repair materials, mortars, coatings, and products conform to the specified standards before they are used.
  • Process monitoring — close control of the critical stages that determine durability, particularly surface preparation, application, and curing.
  • Inspection & testing — regular quality verification throughout the programme to confirm that completed work meets acceptance criteria.
  • Documentation & reporting — complete, traceable record-keeping that maintains transparency from the first pour to final handover.

Why It Matters

Repair work is unforgiving of shortcuts. Contamination left on a prepared surface, an incorrectly batched mortar, or premature loading can compromise a repair long before its intended service life is reached — and by then the cost of putting it right is far higher than the cost of getting it right the first time. Independent supervision catches these issues while they can still be corrected, verifies that the design is faithfully implemented, and gives every stakeholder a defensible record of how the work was executed.

Our Approach

Because CDE is not tied to any contractor or product supplier, our supervision reflects the interests of the structure and its owner alone. We coordinate closely with contractors and stakeholders throughout the works, but our judgement on acceptance remains impartial. Every project is engineer-led, and our reporting is structured so that the quality of the finished repair can be demonstrated — not simply assumed.

Where It Applies

Our supervision and QA/QC services suit concrete repair projects, structural strengthening works, infrastructure maintenance programmes, and industrial and commercial facilities. Wherever the quality of execution will decide the durability of the outcome, independent oversight helps ensure the repair delivers the reliability, service life, and reduced risk of premature failure that its design promised.
